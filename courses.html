Teaching

Teaching Activities

I conduct my teaching activities in the Department of Mathematics at University of Oran 1 Ahmed Ben Bella, across various levels ranging from Bachelor's to Master's programs. My teaching covers both fundamental and advanced topics in computer science, with a focus on programming, algorithms, parallel computing, and scientific computing.

Courses Taught

  • Parallel Computer Architecture and Programming
    • Master 2 — Advanced parallel architectures and programming models (MPI, OpenMP, CUDA)
  • Object-Oriented Programming with C++
    • Master 1 — Object-oriented programming concepts, inheritance, polymorphism, templates, STL
  • Fundamentals of Programming and Scientific Computing with Python
    • L1 Math — Introduction to programming, NumPy, SciPy, Matplotlib, scientific computation
  • Open Source
    • L1 Math — Introduction to open-source software, Linux, version control (Git), open-source licenses
  • Programming Tools for Mathematics
    • L1 Math — Basic programming tools for mathematical applications
  • Programming Tools for Mathematics II
    • L2 Math — Advanced programming tools, numerical methods, and mathematical libraries
  • Advanced data structures and algorithms 2
    • L1 Info — Practical work on advanced data structures and algorithms (trees, graphs, sorting, searching)

Teaching Practices

I teach a wide range of computer science topics to diverse audiences in both bachelor's and master's programs. As a course coordinator, I am responsible for delivering lectures (CM), tutorials (TD), and practical work (TP) for the modules listed above. I design the pedagogical materials, including course slides, exercise sheets, practical lab manuals, and assessment materials, which are distributed online via the university's learning management platform.

In my teaching, I emphasize hands-on learning through practical coding exercises and projects. For programming courses, I use project-based learning approaches where students build real-world applications to reinforce theoretical concepts. I also incorporate collaborative learning strategies, peer programming exercises, and pair work to enhance student engagement and knowledge sharing.

I regularly supervise end-of-year projects and internships for both Bachelor's and Master's students, guiding them through the research process and helping them develop their technical and professional skills.

Pedagogical Responsibilities

Course Coordination

I coordinate the following modules, ensuring alignment between learning objectives, content delivery, and assessment methods:

  • Architecture et programmation des calculateurs parallèles (Master 2)
  • Programmation Orientée objet avec C++ (Master 1)

Student Assessment and Support

I use a combination of formative and summative assessments to evaluate student progress, including written exams, practical lab tests, project presentations, and continuous assessment. I provide regular feedback to students and offer office hours for individual consultation and support.

Continuous Improvement

I actively participate in pedagogical training sessions and workshops offered by the university to stay updated on innovative teaching methodologies. I continually adapt my teaching materials and approaches based on student feedback and learning outcomes to ensure the highest quality of education.

  • Training in project-based learning and active pedagogies
  • Workshops on digital tools for education (Moodle, virtual classrooms)
  • Participation in experience-sharing meetings on teaching innovations
