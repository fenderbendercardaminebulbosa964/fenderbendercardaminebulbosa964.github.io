Research

Research Overview

My research focuses on the development of intelligent systems for bioinformatics, computer vision, and robotics. I am particularly interested in the application of machine learning and evolutionary algorithms to solve complex real-world problems such as cancer classification from microarray data, autonomous vehicle navigation, and object detection.

A central theme of my work is the development of hybrid approaches that combine feature selection methods with optimization algorithms to improve the performance of classification systems in high-dimensional domains. I also explore the integration of explainable AI techniques to make intelligent systems more transparent and trustworthy.

Research Axes

Bioinformatics and Computational Biology

My research in bioinformatics focuses on the analysis of high-dimensional biological data, particularly gene expression data from microarrays. I develop feature selection methods based on mutual information, social spider optimization, and other metaheuristics to identify the most relevant genes for cancer classification. This work aims to improve the accuracy and interpretability of diagnostic models while reducing computational complexity.

Recent contributions include:

  • Hybrid gene selection frameworks combining mutual information with the Social Spider Optimization algorithm
  • Cancer classification approaches using optimized feature subsets from microarray data
  • Comparative studies of evolutionary algorithms for high-dimensional gene selection

Computer Vision and Object Detection

My work in computer vision addresses the challenge of real-time object detection in complex environments. I develop enhanced deep learning architectures, including YOLO-based models, for applications such as urban waste detection and autonomous vehicle perception.

Recent contributions include:

  • Accurate real-time urban waste detection using enhanced YOLOv9-EfficientViT frameworks
  • Multi-sensor approaches for UAV navigation in cluttered environments
  • Integration of attention mechanisms for improved detection accuracy

Robotics and Autonomous Systems

My research in robotics focuses on autonomous navigation and perception for unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs). I work on developing robust vision-based navigation systems that can operate effectively in challenging environments with obstacles and dynamic conditions.

Recent contributions include:

  • BS-CYOLOv5 multi-sensor approaches for enhanced UAV navigation
  • Integration of computer vision with sensor fusion for autonomous systems
  • Real-time obstacle detection and avoidance strategies

Explainable Artificial Intelligence (XAI)

I am actively working on making AI systems more transparent and interpretable. My research explores how feature selection methods and model simplification techniques can contribute to the explainability of machine learning models, particularly in critical domains such as healthcare and autonomous systems.

This includes developing methods to:

  • Interpret the decision-making process of classification models
  • Identify the most influential features in prediction outcomes
  • Provide human-understandable explanations for complex model behaviors
